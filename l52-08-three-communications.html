Quick Check

Knowledge Check
You write a shift handoff note using AI assistance. The AI draft says "all equipment running normally." You know this is not true — you had a sensor fault on Machine 5 earlier that you manually reset, and it ran fine after that. What should you do before sending?
✅ Correct. A sensor fault — even one that was resolved — belongs in the shift handoff note. The incoming shift needs to know it happened, when, what was done, and whether it recurred. "Running normally now" is true, but it hides the history that the next operator needs to watch for. The fix is simple: edit the AI draft to include the Machine 5 incident. That's what human review of AI-generated content is for.
❌ The sensor fault belongs in the written handoff note — not just mentioned verbally. If you only say it verbally, there's no record of it, and if the incoming operator doesn't have time to relay the information to the next person, the history is lost. Starting a new AI prompt would produce another draft that also doesn't mention Machine 5 unless you specifically include it. The right fix is to edit the AI draft to add the Machine 5 incident: what happened, when, what was done, that it ran normally after.

What You Learned in This Article

✓
A shift handoff note must include: what happened this shift, any equipment issues and their status, material or inventory flags, what the incoming shift needs to watch for, and any pending actions. The AI can organize and draft it — you verify every detail.
✓
A maintenance request must include: which equipment (specific ID), what the problem is, when it started, under what conditions it occurs, current operational status, and what action is needed. Missing details force the maintenance tech to investigate before they even start.
✓
A safety alert must include: exact location, what the hazard is, approximate size or severity, what immediate action was taken, and what response is needed. Safety alerts should never wait — send immediately when a hazard is identified.
✓
For all three communication types: gather your specific facts first, build a detailed AI prompt, review the draft carefully for accuracy, edit what's wrong or missing, then send. The AI saves time on structure and phrasing — you ensure accuracy.
