Simulation: You're the Process Improvement Lead — Find the Bottleneck

You have access to a real production floor data dashboard. Analyze the numbers, identify the bottleneck, propose an AI-supported improvement, and explain your reasoning — before seeing what the AI actually flagged.

Your Role and the Situation

You are the Process Improvement Lead at Ridgeline Metal Fab. The operations manager has asked you to review the production floor data for the past four weeks and identify where process improvement investment should be focused. You have access to the AI monitoring dashboard below — the same data the AI is analyzing.

Your job: read the data, form your own analysis, then submit your conclusions. After you submit, the AI will reveal what it flagged — and you'll compare your reasoning to the AI's pattern detection. Your answer and the AI's answer may both be valid, or they may differ in important ways. That's the point.

Your Analysis
What Does the Data Tell You?

Question 1 — Bottleneck Identification
Looking at the dashboard, which station is the primary bottleneck limiting this line's throughput?
Question 2 — Root Cause Hypothesis
The dashboard shows Station 3's cycle time increased from 14.8 to 22.4 minutes over 4 weeks, with the afternoon shift running 36% slower than the morning shift and 17% downtime vs. 8%. What is the most likely hypothesis about the cause?
Question 3 — Improvement Recommendation
Station 6 (QC Inspection) is running 38% above its planned cycle time but shows no queue and only 44% utilization — nobody on the team has flagged it as a concern. Should it be included in the improvement plan?
🤖 AI Process Monitor — What the System Actually Flagged
Comparing Your Analysis to the AI's Findings
AI Primary Finding — Bottleneck Identified
Station 3 (MIG Welding) flagged as primary throughput constraint. AI analysis: actual cycle time 22.4 min vs. 14 min plan = 60% overrun. Queue depth 16.8 units = 8.4x the next-highest station. Utilization 96%. Calculated impact: resolving Station 3 to plan cycle time would increase line throughput from 2.7 to approximately 4.0 parts/hour — a 48% gain. The AI also flagged a secondary finding: cycle time trend shows consistent drift upward across all three shifts over the 4-week window, suggesting a mechanical issue rather than an operator issue as the primary driver.
AI Secondary Finding — Shift Anomaly
Afternoon shift at Station 3 flagged separately. AI analysis: afternoon shift cycle time (26.1 min) and downtime (17%) deviate significantly from morning (19.2 min, 8%) and night (21.8 min, 11%) shifts. The AI cannot determine whether this is due to operator experience, product mix, or a machine issue that worsens over the course of a shift. However, it flags this as a separate investigation priority from the overall drift — the two signals may have different root causes and different fixes.
AI Tertiary Finding — Next Bottleneck Risk
Station 6 (QC Inspection) flagged as secondary concern. AI analysis: current cycle time 9.7 min vs. 7.0 min plan = 38% overrun. Currently masked because throughput from Station 3 only arrives at Station 6 at 2.7 parts/hour — slow enough that Station 6 can handle it with 44% utilization. If Station 3 is fixed to plan (4.3 parts/hour), Station 6 at its current 9.7 min cycle time would become the new bottleneck immediately. The AI recommends investigating Station 6's overrun concurrently with Station 3 work.
How Your Analysis Compares
The AI and a well-trained human analyst looking at the same dashboard should reach the same primary conclusion: Station 3 is the bottleneck and needs immediate investigation. Where the AI adds unique value here is in the quantified throughput impact (48% gain if fixed) and the detection of the cycle time drift trend across 4 weeks — a pattern that's very difficult to spot in daily observation. The Station 6 secondary finding is also a classic "next bottleneck" risk that experienced process engineers know to look for but that the AI calculates automatically from utilization math. Where humans add irreplaceable value: investigating why Station 3 is slow (mechanical? training? product mix?), evaluating whether adding a second welder is feasible (workspace, certification availability, budget), and having the conversations with the afternoon shift team that will surface what's actually happening during those slower hours.
