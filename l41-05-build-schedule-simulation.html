Your Scenario: Monday Morning at Ridgeline Manufacturing

It's 6:45 AM on Monday. You're the production planner at Ridgeline Manufacturing, a mid-size metal fabrication shop in Ohio. One job just got authorized for today: Work Order #WO-2204, a batch of 100 custom aluminum mounting plates for a robotics company.

The customer deadline is today at 5:00 PM. This is a same-day job — tight, but doable if the schedule is solid. Your job in this simulation is to build the schedule yourself, then compare your plan to what the AI suggests.

What This Simulation Teaches

✓
Building a schedule from scratch — even for a small 5-task job — requires tracking worker certifications, machine availability, task dependencies, and time slot conflicts simultaneously. This is why AI tools are valuable.
✓
The AI suggestion isn't automatically better than a human plan — it's different, based on optimization goals you may or may not share. Comparing both plans develops your judgment about when to follow AI and when to push back.
✓
Critical planning details — like Sam being new and slower, or Riley not being available until 9 AM — matter enormously. If those facts aren't in the system, the AI doesn't know them.
✓
The right answer isn't always "accept the AI plan" or "reject it" — often the best response is a thoughtful combination: accept parts of the AI's optimization while preserving the human context it missed.
