The Pre-Send Checklist: Is It Accurate? Is It Clear? Is It Right for the Audience?

Three questions that should pass through your mind before every work message you send. This article turns the checklist slide into a full practice session — including four real messages to evaluate yourself.

Why a Checklist Matters

Most communication mistakes in manufacturing happen in the last 30 seconds before sending. The content is basically right, but a key detail is wrong. The tone is off. The message goes to the wrong person. The urgency level is misjudged.

A pre-send checklist is not about slowing you down — it takes about 20 seconds. It's about creating a moment of intentional review between "I wrote this" and "I sent this." That pause is where mistakes get caught.

This is even more important when you've used AI to draft the message. AI drafts can have errors you didn't put in. A pre-send check catches them before they become your professional problem.

Quick Check

Knowledge Check
Before sending a maintenance request you drafted using AI, you run the pre-send checklist and realize the AI included a detail that isn't accurate — it said the noise started "yesterday afternoon" but it actually started this morning. What do you do?
✅ Correct. The timing detail matters more than it might seem: "yesterday afternoon" tells the maintenance team this is a slow-developing issue that can be scheduled; "this morning" tells them it's new and may be more urgent. That distinction affects prioritization. Fixing one phrase takes 5 seconds. Sending an inaccurate message can cause the wrong response. Always fix it before sending — that's what the pre-send checklist is for.
❌ The timing matters. "Yesterday afternoon" vs. "this morning" tells the maintenance team whether this is an escalating issue or something brand new — which affects how they prioritize the response. You always fix inaccurate details before sending, no matter how minor they seem. Sending with a disclaimer that the AI might be wrong undermines your credibility and doesn't actually protect you professionally. Fix it, then send it.

What You Learned in This Article

✓
The pre-send checklist has seven questions covering accuracy, clarity, audience appropriateness, tone, completeness, timing, and next steps. Running through them takes about 20 seconds and catches most communication mistakes before they become problems.
✓
Accuracy is non-negotiable — even small details matter. "Yesterday" vs. "this morning," "Line 3" vs. "Line 4," "warning" vs. "error" — these distinctions change how people respond to your message.
✓
Audience appropriateness means asking whether the right person is getting this message, at the right level of detail, in the right format. A message to maintenance and a message to a supervisor about the same issue should look different.
✓
The checklist is especially important for AI-drafted messages, because AI can introduce inaccuracies you didn't put in. The human review step is what makes AI-assisted communication trustworthy.
