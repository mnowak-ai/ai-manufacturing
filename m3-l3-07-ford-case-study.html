The Dearborn Truck Plant: Scale First

Before you can understand what the AI does, you need to understand what it's operating inside. The Ford Dearborn Truck Plant is one of the most productive manufacturing facilities in North America — and its product is America's best-selling vehicle for over 40 consecutive years.

At that volume and price point, defects that reach customers are enormously costly. A single recall covering even 1% of annual production means 7,500 vehicles — each requiring dealer service, customer communication, and parts replacement. Catching defects at the line, before a truck leaves the plant, is both a quality and financial imperative.

What "Millimeter-Scale" Detection Actually Means

Ford's AI inspection systems detect defects smaller than 1mm — roughly the width of a pencil tip. The human eye reliably detects surface defects of about 3–5mm under good lighting. That gap matters at production speed.

📏 Why Sub-Millimeter Detection Matters

A paint inclusion that's 0.8mm — invisible at arm's length under factory lighting — becomes clearly visible in direct sunlight at a specific angle. A customer paying $60,000 for a pickup truck notices it on Day 1. The warranty repair costs Ford far more than catching it at the line.

👁️ Human Inspector

Fatigues after hours of repetitive visual work. Detection accuracy drops 20–30% by end of shift. Subjective — different inspectors make different calls. Can cover each panel in 30–60 seconds at best.

📷 AI Vision System

No fatigue. Same sensitivity at hour 8 as hour 1. Objective — identical criteria every time. Inspects each surface in milliseconds. Generates data on every defect found for continuous improvement.

The Two AI Systems: AiTriz and MAIVS

AiTriz

AI-Powered Problem Solving Platform

Role: Engineering root cause analysis and solution generation

AiTriz is built on the TRIZ methodology — a structured engineering problem-solving framework. Ford's AI-powered version analyzes recurring quality problems and searches millions of engineering patents and case studies to surface solutions. When a specific weld keeps cracking or a paint defect pattern keeps recurring, AiTriz can generate solution candidates in hours rather than the weeks a traditional engineering investigation would take.

Root cause analysis acceleration

MAIVS

Machine Aided Industrial Vision System

Role: Real-time surface and assembly defect detection

MAIVS is Ford's computer vision platform for real-time defect detection. High-resolution cameras at key inspection points capture images of vehicle surfaces, welds, panels, and components as they move through the line. An AI model trained on thousands of good and defective examples analyzes each image in real time — generating alerts with defect location, type, and confidence score that go immediately to quality technicians.

What Workers Experience on the Line

1

Vehicle enters inspection zone

As each truck body passes through a painted surface inspection station, MAIVS cameras capture hundreds of images from multiple angles. The full surface scan takes seconds — far faster than any human inspection.

2

AI flags a potential defect

MAIVS detects a paint inclusion on the driver-side door. Alert appears on the technician's workstation: location (door panel, lower third), defect type (inclusion), confidence (94%), and a magnified image of the flagged area.

3

Technician verifies the finding

The quality technician goes to the flagged location, uses an inspection light at the angle that best reveals surface defects, and confirms: yes, there is a paint inclusion. The AI was correct.

4

Technician makes the call

The inclusion is 0.8mm — within the range that can be polished out. The technician tags the vehicle for paint correction at the refinish station and logs the defect. The truck continues down the line with a work order attached.

5

Data feeds back to the system

The confirmed defect — location, type, size, shift, and paint batch — is logged. Quality management software tracks whether inclusions cluster on specific shifts, batches, or conditions. This drives root cause investigation and prevents recurrence.

The AI catches the defect. The technician confirms it. The technician decides what to do. Quality data from both feeds back into the system to prevent the next one. That's the full loop.
