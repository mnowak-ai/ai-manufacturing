By the end of this article, you'll know exactly how to use AI as a communication assistant — not as a replacement for your judgment, but as a tool that helps you produce clearer, more professional writing faster. The AI drafts. You own it.

Using AI to help write a work message is completely professional — the same way using spell-check is professional. What matters is that you read it, verify it's accurate, and take responsibility for what you send.

The 5-Step Process

Every time you use AI to help draft a work communication, follow these five steps in order. Never skip Step 3 or Step 4 — those are where your professional judgment does its most important work.

Build Your Own Prompt

The quality of what AI produces depends almost entirely on the quality of what you put in. A vague prompt produces a vague draft. A specific prompt — with the situation, audience, and tone — produces something close to what you actually need. Use this tool to build a strong prompt for your situation.

Quick Check

Knowledge Check
A quality inspector uses AI to draft a shift handoff note. The AI draft says "all equipment is running normally." But she actually noticed that the vision system had an elevated false positive rate in the last hour of the shift. What should she do?
✅ Correct. The AI produced a draft based on limited information — it didn't know about the elevated false positive rate because she didn't tell it. The fix is simple: edit the inaccurate phrase and add the specific information that needs to be there. This is exactly what "draft → review → correct → send" looks like in practice. The AI saved her time structuring the message. She's responsible for making sure what she sends is accurate. That division of labor is the correct one.
❌ The right move is to edit the draft — fix what's wrong and add what's missing. Sending an inaccurate message puts the outgoing shift at risk of missing important information. Starting over from scratch wastes the value of the AI draft. Trying again with a different prompt won't help because the AI still won't know about the false positive rate unless she tells it. The human review step exists exactly for situations like this: catch what the AI got wrong, and fix it before sending.

What You Learned in This Article

✓
The 5-step process for using AI to draft work communications: Gather your facts → Build your prompt → Get the draft → Review for accuracy and tone → Send (or edit and send).
✓
The quality of an AI draft depends on the quality of the prompt. A specific prompt — with the situation, audience, and tone — produces a better draft than a vague one.
✓
AI drafts often need editing: wrong details, missing context, wrong tone, or phrases that sound too generic. Human review is not optional — it's the most important step in the process.
✓
You own everything you send. Using AI to draft is a tool. Sending an inaccurate message is your professional responsibility, not the AI's. Review every draft before sending.
