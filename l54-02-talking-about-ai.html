How to Talk About AI on Your Résumé and in Job Interviews

This is the most career-accelerating article in the certification. How you describe what you know is almost as important as what you know. This article gives you the exact language — for your résumé, for the interview room, and for conversations with your current supervisor.

Why Language Matters as Much as Knowledge

Two workers can have exactly the same skills and get very different outcomes from a hiring process — because one of them can articulate what they know and the other can't. Knowledge that you can't describe clearly is invisible to the people making decisions about you.

This is especially true for AI skills in manufacturing, because most candidates in the job market right now either say nothing about AI (underusing what they know) or overclaim AI experience they don't have (which falls apart immediately in an interview). The sweet spot — specific, accurate, appropriately humble — is where this certification puts you.

When someone can tell me specifically what they know about AI in manufacturing — not 'I'm comfortable with technology' but the actual functions, the actual judgment calls, the actual limits — that's immediately impressive. That kind of specificity is rare.

— Manufacturing HR Director

AI skills belong in three places on a résumé: the skills section (with specific tool and system knowledge), the experience section (as action-verb bullet points tied to outcomes), and the certifications section (with the full certification name).
